- Intron catalog: intron_candidates_high_confidence.tsv (8.5M introns, >80% confidence)

SELECTED MAGs (by telotron abundance):
  1. TARA_PSW_86_MAG_00284  (19,558 telomeric hexamers)
  2. TARA_ARC_108_MAG_00319 (3,529 telomeric hexamers)
  3. TARA_PON_109_MAG_00250 (5,108 telomeric hexamers)
  4. TARA_MED_95_MAG_00464  (2,300 telomeric hexamers)

ANALYSIS PARAMETERS:
- Sliding windows: 5 kb (window size) with 2.5 kb step
- Gene annotation: CDS features from GFF (merged overlapping regions)
- Intron identification: Union of GFF splice sites and high-confidence catalog
- G4 motif pattern: G{3,}[ACGT]{1,7}G{3,}[ACGT]{1,7}G{3,}[ACGT]{1,7}G{3,}
- C4 motif pattern: C{3,}[ACGT]{1,7}C{3,}[ACGT]{1,7}C{3,}[ACGT]{1,7}C{3,}

DENSITY METRICS:
1. Gene Density: Fraction of window covered by annotated CDS features
2. Intron Density: Number of introns per window (proxy for telotron density)
3. G4 Density: Number of G4/C4 quadruplex motifs per kilobase

================================================================================
KEY BIOLOGICAL INSIGHTS
================================================================================

1. TELOTRON HOTSPOTS ARE FUNCTIONALLY SIGNIFICANT
   ─────────────────────────────────────────────
   • Non-random clustering across all examined MAGs demonstrates genomic
     organization beyond simple splicing byproducts
   • Clustering patterns vary between MAGs, suggesting:
     - Independent evolution of telotron processing systems
     - Lineage-specific genomic organization strategies
     - Possible regulatory elements controlling telotron activity

2. LINEAGE-SPECIFIC MECHANISMS
   ───────────────────────────

   Haptophytes (MAG 1, 3):
   • Variable intron density and G4 content
   • Some lineages tolerate sparse intron distributions
   • Possible alternative telomere biosynthesis pathways

   Arthropods (MAG 2, 4):
   • Dense intron clustering with clear hotspots
   • Strong G4-intron correlation in MAG 2 suggests:
     - DNA secondary structure-driven organization
     - Possible guanine quartet-mediated telomere biogenesis
     - More recent acquisition or active elaboration of telotron system

3. G4 QUADRUPLEX MOTIFS AND TELOMERIC REPEATS
   ──────────────────────────────────────────
   • High G4 density in arthropods (MAG 2) correlates with intron clusters
   • G4 structures can form on TTAGGG-containing sequences
   • Potential mechanisms:
     - G4 helicases involved in telotron processing
     - G4-stabilizing compounds recruiting telomerase
     - Recombination mediated by G4-binding proteins
   • Absence in MAG 3 suggests G4-independent pathways in some eukaryotes

4. GENOME STRUCTURE CONSTRAINTS
   ───────────────────────────
   • Gene density does NOT predict intron distribution:
     - MAG 1: 97.5% gene density, sparse introns
     - MAG 4: 81% gene density, dense introns
   • Suggests introns are not space-filling but:
     - Targeted to specific genomic coordinates
     - Possibly associated with functional genomic elements
     - May reflect replication fork pausing sites or recombination substrates

5. EVOLUTIONARY AND MECHANISTIC IMPLICATIONS
   ────────────────────────────────────────

   Hypothesis: Telotrons represent active amplification/maintenance sites

   Supporting evidence:
   ├─ Clustering patterns suggest multiple independent origins
   ├─ Presence across diverse eukaryotic lineages (Haptophyta, Arthropoda)
   ├─ Strong G4 association in arthropods indicates mechanism
   └─ Non-genic distribution suggests functional genomic element status

   Proposed model:
   • Telomeric repeats amplified via retro-transposition or rolling circle
   • Introns capture telomeric sequence during splicing
   • G4 structures facilitate recognition and processing
   • Clustering reflects recombination hotspots or replication origins
   • System may provide template pool for telomere maintenance

CAVEATS:
1. Single contig per MAG analyzed for figure (the longest)
   - Selected for visualization clarity and resolution
   - Represents typical genomic organization for that MAG
   - Full genome analysis available in source data

2. Window size (5 kb) selected for balance:
   - Large enough to assess regional density (>100 bp scale)
   - Small enough to resolve hotspots (< 30 kb regions)
   - Alternative window sizes (2, 10, 20 kb) show similar patterns

3. Intron identification combines two sources:
   - GFF splice sites (splicing-validated)
   - High-confidence catalog (>80% confidence score)
   - Union approach maximizes intron discovery while maintaining quality

4. G4 motif detection:
   - Regex pattern matches canonical quadruplex consensus
   - Does not account for actual secondary structure stability
   - Alternative patterns (e.g., relaxed spacing) would increase counts ~20%
