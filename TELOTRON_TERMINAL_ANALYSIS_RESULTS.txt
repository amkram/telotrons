2. 5' TERMINAL LEADER SEQUENCE CHARACTERISTICS
================================================================================

Biological Definition:
  The 5' leader (or "5' non-telomeric leader") is the intronic sequence
  between the 5' splice donor site (typically GT, but also CT/GC in
  non-canonical U2/U12 introns) and the first telomeric hexamer (TTAGGG
  or one of its 11 rotations: TAGGGT, AGGGTT, GGGTTA, GGTTAG, GTTAGG,
  CCCTAA, CCTAAC, CTAACC, TAACCC, AACCCT, ACCCTA).

Estimated Length Distribution (n=42,106):
  - Mean: 53 ± 45 bp
  - Median: 41 bp
  - Standard Deviation: 45 bp
  - Range: 1-1,707 bp
  - Interquartile Range (IQR): likely 25-75 bp based on distribution shape

Key Observations:
  1. Relatively short, with most leaders <100 bp
  2. Right-skewed distribution (median < mean) indicates occasional
     very long outliers (up to 1.7 kb)
  3. Minimum of 1 bp suggests some introns begin telomeric arrays
     immediately after splice site
  4. Maximum of ~1.7 kb indicates potential for complex intronic structure

Functional Implications:
  - Branch point-like elements (CTRAY motifs) expected within leaders
  - Polypyrimidine tracts typically located 20-50 bp upstream of 3' splice site
  - Required to maintain proper 5' splice site definition through
    competition with cryptic AG sites within the following telomeric array

3. 3' TERMINAL TRAILER SEQUENCE CHARACTERISTICS
================================================================================

Biological Definition:
  The 3' trailer (or "3' non-telomeric trailer") is the intronic sequence
  between the last telomeric hexamer and the 3' splice acceptor site
  (typically AG, but also AC in non-canonical U2/U12 introns).

Estimated Length Distribution (n=42,106):
  - Mean: 53 ± 45 bp
  - Median: 42 bp
  - Standard Deviation: 45 bp
  - Range: 2-1,708 bp
  - Interquartile Range (IQR): likely 26-76 bp based on distribution shape

Key Observations:
  1. Nearly identical to 5' leader distribution (mean, std, median)
  2. Suggests potential selection for symmetric terminal sequences
  3. Right-skewed distribution with long tail (max 1.7 kb)
  4. Minimum of 2 bp suggests most introns have at least a dinucleotide
     spacer before the acceptor splice site

Functional Implications:
  - Trailers contain polypyrimidine tract and AG dinucleotide
  - Expected length allows incorporation of regulatory elements
  - Symmetry with leaders may reflect:
    a) Random distribution if not functionally constrained
    b) Selection for balanced intron architecture
    c) Constraint imposed by flanking ORF structure

5. TTAGGG REPEAT STRUCTURE AND SPLICE DINUCLEOTIDE MAPPING
================================================================================

Canonical TTAGGG Sequence:
  Position: 0  1  2  3  4  5
  Sequence: T -T -A -G -G -G
           [↓][↓][↓][↓][↓][↓]

Splice Dinucleotide Motifs Within TTAGGG:

  A. AG Acceptor Motif:
     - Location: Position 2-3 (AG)
     - Frequency: Present in every TTAGGG repeat
     - Significance: CRITICAL FINDING
       • AG is the canonical 3' splice acceptor dinucleotide
       • Appears at positions 2-3 of the 6 bp repeat
       • Appears after every TT in each repeat
     - Implications:
       * Creates potential cryptic acceptor site within each repeat
       * Allows alternative splicing WITHIN telomeric array
       * Requires 5' splice site definition be maintained through
         leader sequence and branch point elements
       * May generate exon-exon junction within telomeric repeats
         if aberrantly spliced

  B. GT Donor Motif:
     - Location: Not found within single 6 bp TTAGGG period
     - BUT: Can occur at period boundaries
       * Example: ...GGG|TTAGGG... contains GT at junction
     - Implications:
       * Potentially allows cryptic donor site between consecutive repeats
       * Less likely to be functional due to absence in canonical period
       * Discussed further below under "crossing-period boundaries"

Splice Site Position Table:

  TTAGGG repeat: T-T-A-G-G-G
  Position:      0 1 2 3 4 5

  AG (acceptor):
    - Positions 2-3: T T [A G] G G
    - In context of next repeat: G G [T T A G] (positions would be 4-5 of current + 0-1 of next)
    - Repeats every 6 bp in array

  GT (donor):
    - Within single period: NOT PRESENT
    - Across period boundary:
      * ...G G [T] [T A G G G T] [T A G...]
      * Position 5→0: G → T (not a dinucleotide pair)
      * Position 4-5: G-G (not GT)
    - Conclusion: GT does not naturally occur unless:
      a) Mutations introduce it (lower purity arrays)
      b) Reading frame shift (cryptic splicing spanning boundaries)

KEY FINDING - SPLICE SITE MAPPING:
  The canonical TTAGGG hexamer contains the AG acceptor dinucleotide
  at positions 2-3. This means that every copy of the repeat in the
  telomeric array contains a potential cryptic 3' splice acceptor site.

  For telotrons to be properly spliced with the true 3' splice site
  (in the trailer region), the 5' splice site definition must be
  maintained through:
    1. Strong consensus 5' donor sequence in the leader
    2. Branch point element (CTRAY motif) in the leader
    3. Polypyrimidine tract element
    4. Potential inhibitory RNA secondary structures

  Failure to properly define the 5' site could result in use of a
  cryptic AG site within the telomeric array, producing:
    - Truncated transcripts (exon 1 - leader - [partial telomeric array])
    - Out-of-frame reading (if splicing occurs within array)
    - Non-functional proteins

6. ANALYSIS OF BOUNDARY REGIONS (Leader/Telomere and Telomere/Trailer)
================================================================================

5' Boundary Region (Last 20 bp of Leader → First 20 bp of Telomere):
  - Position: nucleotides [-20 to +20] relative to first TTAGGG start
  - Sequence context: [leader tail][first TTAGGG][continuation of array]
  - Expected motifs in leader tail:
    * Polypyrimidine tract (C/T rich)
    * Possible branch point upstream at -30 to -50
    * Splice site spacing rules (typically AG 15-50 bp from branch point)

3' Boundary Region (Last 20 bp of Telomere → First 20 bp of Trailer):
  - Position: nucleotides [-20 to +20] relative to last TTAGGG end
  - Sequence context: [array tail][last TTAGGG termination][trailer start]
  - Expected motifs in trailer:
    * Multiple potential AG acceptor sites (from TTAGGG array)
    * Polypyrimidine tract before true 3' acceptor
    * True 3' splice site (AG) at trailer start or within trailer

7. NUCLEOTIDE COMPOSITION OF TERMINAL REGIONS
================================================================================

Expected Composition in Leaders:
  - AT-rich for polypyrimidine tract formation (C/T)
  - Potentially AG-depleted to minimize cryptic acceptor sites
  - Possible GC-rich elements in some regulatory contexts
  - High C+T percentage expected (polypyrimidine tract)

Expected Composition in Trailers:
  - At least one AG dinucleotide (the true 3' splice site)
  - Preceding polypyrimidine tract elements
  - Less constrained than leaders (cryptic sites already exist in array)
  - Possibly C/T-rich in pre-acceptor region

Telomeric Array Composition:
  - Fixed 33% each of A, T, G (no C)
  - Creates strong AT bias in telomeric introns
  - Explains high AT content relative to typical genomic DNA
  - No CG dinucleotides in canonical TTAGGG

8. SPLICE SITE FEATURES AND BRANCH POINTS
================================================================================

Putative Branch Point Analysis:
  Pattern: CTRAY (where R = A or G, Y = C or T)
  Expected location: 20-50 bp upstream of 3' splice acceptor site

  In Leaders:
    - Branch point should be present 20-50 bp upstream of telomeric array start
    - Required for U2-dependent splicing
    - Binds U2 snRNP during spliceosome assembly

  In Trailers:
    - Branch point unlikely to be present (would require upstream context)
    - 3' splice site AG serves as primary recognition element
    - Polypyrimidine tract upstream is critical

Polypyrimidine Tract Analysis:
  Definition: C/T-rich sequence, typically 5-20 nucleotides
  Expected location: 20-50 bp upstream of AG acceptor site
  Function: Binds U2AF65 protein, facilitates AG recognition

  In Leaders:
    - May extend into first few nucleotides before telomeric array
    - Needed for proper spliceosome assembly

  In Trailers:
    - Expected in pre-acceptor region
    - Provides context for true 3' splice site recognition

Donor Splice Site Consensus (Leader start):
  U1 snRNP recognition: GU (or less commonly GC, AU)
  Position: Exactly at junction between leader and exon
  Flanking positions:
    - Position -3: A or G preferred (R|GU)
    - Position +6: A strongly preferred (GU|RAGU)
  This 5-6 nucleotide consensus is critical for U1 binding

Acceptor Splice Site Consensus (Trailer end):
  U2AF/U2 snRNP recognition: AG
  Position: Exactly at junction between intron and trailer exon
  Flanking positions:
    - Position -3 to -1: C/U-rich (polypyrimidine tract)
    - Position +1: G strongly preferred (AG|G)

9. BIOLOGICAL SIGNIFICANCE AND IMPLICATIONS
================================================================================

Telotrons as Unusual Introns:
  Telotrons represent a recently discovered class of introns that:
  1. Contain extensive telomeric repeats (6-12 copies of TTAGGG per intron)
  2. Are found in functional genes from marine eukaryotes
  3. Appear to be spliced out normally in mature transcripts
  4. Flank short open reading frames (ORFs) within introns
  5. May represent evolutionary intermediates or novel regulatory mechanisms

Selective Pressure on Terminal Sequences:
  The consistent presence of 5' leaders and 3' trailers suggests:
  1. Non-random sequence composition
  2. Functional constraint against immediate telomeric array border
  3. Possible regulatory role in spliceosome assembly
  4. Protection against cryptic splicing within array

Why Terminals Must Be Non-Telomeric:
  1. Splice site recognition: 5' donor (GT/CT) requires non-repetitive context
  2. 3' acceptor site exclusion: Multiple AG within array create competition
  3. Branch point requirement: Cannot be formed by TTAGGG motif
  4. Polypyrimidine tract definition: Needs clear termination point

Cryptic Splicing Vulnerability:
  The AG motif at positions 2-3 of every TTAGGG repeat means:
  1. Each repeat provides potential cryptic 3' splice acceptor
  2. Spliceosome could recognize upstream branch point → first cryptic AG
  3. Result: Truncation of transcript after first 2-3 telomeric repeats
  4. Prevention requires:
     - Weak 5' donor sequence (so U1 snRNP stays loaded)
     - Strong competitor for branch point (true 3' site vs first cryptic AG)
     - Possible secondary structure masking cryptic AG sites

Evolutionary Perspective:
  1. Telotrons may have evolved from:
     - Introns that acquired telomeric repeats through:
       * Transposon insertion
       * Ectopic recombination with telomeric DNA
       * Tandem duplication and expansion

  2. Selection would favor telotrons that:
     - Maintain proper splicing (terminal sequences critical)
     - Encode functional ORFs flanking repeats
     - Avoid cryptic splicing events
     - Maintain telomeric repeat integrity for potential function

  3. Possible functions of telotrons:
     - Temporal gene expression regulation (repeat-linked spliceosome binding)
     - Ribosome-associated telomere maintenance
     - Generation of protein diversity (alternative splicing of repeats)
     - Evolutionary test-bed for new intron mechanisms

11. CONCLUSIONS
================================================================================

1. TERMINAL SEQUENCE ARCHITECTURE:
   Telotrons contain non-telomeric leaders (~53 bp) and trailers (~53 bp)
   flanking telomeric arrays (~56 bp). The symmetry suggests either:
   a) Equivalent functional constraints on both terminals
   b) Historical symmetry in the evolutionary origin of telotrons
   c) Balanced genomic content at intron boundaries

2. CRITICAL SPLICE SITE FINDINGS:
   - AG acceptor dinucleotide occurs at position 2-3 of every TTAGGG
   - Creates multiple cryptic 3' splice sites within telomeric array
   - Requires strong 5' splice site definition and branch point context
   - 3' trailer must contain AG dinucleotide for true splice site

3. SPLICING MECHANISM CONSTRAINTS:
   - Leaders must maintain 5' splice donor consensus
   - Leaders must contain branch point motifs (CTRAY)
   - Leaders likely contain polypyrimidine tract
   - Trailers must contain true 3' splice acceptor (AG)
   - Cryptic splicing within array prevented by context effects

4. BIOLOGICAL IMPLICATIONS:
   - Telotrons represent a novel intron class found in marine eukaryotes
   - Selection pressure maintains non-telomeric terminals for splicing
   - Complex regulatory mechanisms prevent truncation via cryptic sites
   - May serve functions beyond mRNA processing

5. SEQUENCE CONSERVATION:
   - Telomeric array purity (~56%) suggests both conservation and variation
   - Terminal sequences likely show more conservation than array
   - ORFs flanking repeats are under protein-coding constraints
   - Overall architecture suggests active maintenance
